About Sangala Studio

Sangala Studio is a Digital Fabrication tool developed to support the course, Introduction to Design through Making. The word “sangala” means joy in the Lugisu language spoken in the Mt. Elgon region in East Africa. A version of this course focused on exploring art and engineering is being developed to support a collaboration with East Africa. A book, Design through Making: Art and Engineering, has also been developed to support the course. It focuses on a sequence of activities that move from two-dimensional art to three-dimensional design and then incorporate animated and automated mechanisms.

This work is anchored through a collaboration between the Make to Learn Laboratory at the University of Virginia and REACH for Uganda, a non-profit organization registered in Arlington, Virginia. The LEGO figure of an African buffalo was designed and constructed by Moses Kumenya, the Sangala Innovation Teacher who is piloting the section of the course taught in Africa.
